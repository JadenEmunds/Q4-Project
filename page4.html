Jef Cablog


When I started with my art career, it was only natural for me to paint about the traditional culture of Cordillera, the mountainous region in Northern Philippines where my hometown, Barlig, Mountain Province is located. Nature is a dominant aspect of my paintings because it is where I draw most inspiration from. I spent most of my childhood in Barligâ€™s lush forests, playing, climbing its mossy trees or collecting wild berries with other village children. I have also enjoyed hours of swimming and fishing in its creeks, streams and rivers. These pleasant experiences and the bond I have developed with these elements of nature, have always given me happiness, contentment and peace of mind which I hope would be reflected to my audiences and would inspire them whenever they look at my paintings. The culture aspect of my work is part of my identity, which I hope would provide understanding and awareness of my culture, but I hope that even without an indepth knowledge of my culture, people can relate to my paintings with the understanding that all of us have to look to our ancestors past and be reminded of how they have coexisted well with nature.

Initially, I mainly used paintbrushes but back in 2009, I experimented on using palette knives only. Not only is it more convenient to change from one color to another but also easier to clean only by wiping off the paint in the knife and without using any solvents (safe from any side effects with long term use of solvents). Aside from achieving the texture and thickness I want, I also gained speed. I have not used paintbrushes since then.

Location: Barlig, Mountain Province
